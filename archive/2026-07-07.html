New Products / Approvals

Visby Medical's At-Home Flu + COVID PCR Test Clears FDA

New Products/Approvals

Core Development: Visby Medical won FDA clearance (July 2) for its Flu and COVID-19 Test, the first at-home, multi-viral PCR test. It's a single-use, palm-sized cartridge that gives lab-quality results for COVID and flu A/B in about 30 minutes, no lab or clinician required.

Context & Impact: At-home rapid antigen tests are fast but not very accurate; PCR is accurate but usually means a lab and a days-long wait. Visby collapses that trade-off into a single consumer product. For patients, that means same-day antiviral treatment decisions (like Tamiflu or Paxlovid, which only work early) without a clinic visit. For the market, it's a shot at converting flu-season urgent care traffic into retail pharmacy sales.

Technical & Regulatory: Cleared via 510(k), and this is Visby's second OTC PCR product after its standalone COVID/flu single-tests, so the regulatory path was well-trodden. The main challenge ahead is manufacturing scale-up and retail distribution before this fall/winter respiratory season.

Neo Medical Launches "Instant MIS Access" Spine Platform in the US

New Products/Approvals

Core Development: Neo Medical debuted its Instant MIS Access platform (July 6) for minimally invasive spine surgery, covering posterior thoracic, lumbar and sacral procedures.

Context & Impact: Minimally invasive spine surgery has been held back less by surgical technique and more by clunky, multi-vendor instrument sets and OR logistics. Neo Medical is targeting those operational barriers directly — lowering the bar for more hospitals to offer MIS spine surgery instead of open procedures.

Technical & Regulatory: A US commercial launch, not a novel implant, so the near-term hurdle is surgeon training and hospital procurement rather than a new FDA review.

Egg Medical Debuts Lighter, Construction-Free Radiation Shielding for Cath Labs

New Products/Approvals

Core Development: Egg Medical launched EggNest Complete 2.0 (July 6), the next generation of its radiation-shielding system for interventional labs, with a lighter carbon-fiber build and no-construction installation.

Context & Impact: Radiation exposure is a real occupational hazard for interventional cardiologists; traditional shielding means heavy lead aprons or expensive lab renovation. A shield that installs without construction downtime lets hospitals upgrade protection without taking a cath lab offline for weeks.

Technical & Regulatory: An iterative hardware upgrade rather than a new clearance category, so adoption is mostly about hospital capital budgets, not regulatory review.

Tech Innovations

CliniComp Wins Clearance for AI-Native PACS Viewer

Tech Innovations

Core Development: CliniComp received FDA 510(k) clearance (July 7) for its PACS Viewer, classified as a Medical Image Management and Processing System, which folds diagnostic-quality imaging and AI tools directly into its "New Era" EHR.

Context & Impact: Most hospitals bounce between a separate imaging viewer (PACS) and their EHR, with AI tools bolted on as yet another system. Baking image viewing and native AI into the EHR itself bets that clinicians want one screen, not three — part of a broader industry push to consolidate imaging AI into core clinical workflow.

Technical & Regulatory: Cleared under 510(k), a well-established device category. Adoption hinges on hospitals already running CliniComp's EHR being willing to switch imaging viewers.

Clinical Advances

New LAAC Device Matches Watchman at One Year in SAFE-PROTECT Trial

Clinical Advances

Core Development: Twelve-month results from the SAFE-PROTECT trial (published July 6) showed the AnchorMan left atrial appendage closure device was noninferior to the market-leading Watchman 2.5 in AFib patients, with 98.1% clinical success in both arms.

Context & Impact: Watchman has effectively owned the LAAC market for years. A device that matches its safety and efficacy — with less peridevice leakage, a known Watchman weak spot — gives cardiologists a real second option for the first time in a while.

Technical & Regulatory: Death, stroke, bleeding and complication rates were comparable between arms; the differentiator was superior appendage sealing from AnchorMan's anchor-based design. Broader adoption depends on a planned head-to-head trial against the newer Watchman FLX/FLX Pro.

Regulatory Updates

First Urine-Based Multi-Cancer Test Gets FDA Breakthrough Designation

Regulatory Updates

Core Development: TOBY Inc. received FDA Breakthrough Device Designation (July 6) for its urine-based Multi-Cancer Early Detection test — the first urine MCED technology to earn that status.

Context & Impact: Multi-cancer early detection has so far meant a blood draw (Grail's Galleri being the best-known example). A non-invasive urine-based alternative could lower the barrier to routine screening — easier to collect, ship, and fit into a wellness visit.

Technical & Regulatory: Breakthrough designation isn't a clearance — it's priority-review status. TOBY still needs clinical validation data showing urine-based biomarkers can match blood-based sensitivity.

Laplace Interventional Gets FDA Green Light to Start Pivotal Tricuspid Valve Trial

Regulatory Updates

Core Development: Laplace Interventional announced FDA approval of an Investigational Device Exemption for TRIUMPH, its pivotal trial for a transcatheter tricuspid valve replacement system treating severe tricuspid regurgitation.

Context & Impact: Tricuspid valve disease has long been the "forgotten" valve — undertreated because open-heart surgery is high-risk and transcatheter options are newer than for mitral or aortic. TRIUMPH is a direct bid to expand who's eligible for a minimally invasive fix.

Technical & Regulatory: IDE approval just clears Laplace to start enrolling (up to 75 sites); a PMA submission and approval are still years out, in an increasingly crowded field.

Belgian Startup Azalea Vision Lands Up to €7.5M for Smart Contact Lens

Market Developments

Core Development: Azalea Vision was selected for the EU's European Innovation Council Accelerator (announced July 1), securing up to €7.5 million to move its medical-grade smart contact lens into clinical development.

Context & Impact: A small deal in dollar terms, but a signal worth watching: European deep-tech medtech is increasingly funded through public-private EIC vehicles, and "smart" ophthalmic devices are edging toward real clinical testing rather than concept demos.

Technical & Regulatory: The funding moves the lens into clinical trials, not yet pivotal patient use — and EU MDR clearance, a longer path than FDA 510(k) for novel device classes, is still ahead.

It was a genuinely quiet week for device news — no blockbuster M&A closed and no major recall dominated headlines — but the developments that did land point in a consistent direction: incumbency is getting challenged on multiple fronts at once. The other throughline is non-invasive, at-home diagnostics quietly maturing — Visby's at-home PCR and TOBY's urine-based cancer test are both attempts to move testing that used to require a lab or blood draw into something closer to a consumer product.

Practically: hospital buyers should watch EggNest and Neo Medical as low-friction, no-new-clearance-risk upgrades; device investors should keep an eye on the still-wide-open tricuspid valve space; and diagnostics watchers should track whether TOBY's urine MCED data can match blood-based sensitivity — the make-or-break question for the whole approach.
